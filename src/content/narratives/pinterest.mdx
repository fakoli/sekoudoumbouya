## The Story

I've played three roles at once throughout my time here: architect, operator, and translator. Architect, because I design durable systems and standards. Operator, because I show up in incidents and build the tooling that makes operational reality visible. Translator, because none of this matters unless leadership and cross-functional partners can make good decisions with the information we provide. That combination defines my trajectory from Staff to Senior Staff Tech Lead.

### 2021: Building the Foundation

I joined as the technical center of gravity for Cloud Architecture. The team needed someone who could connect the dots across engineering, security, and leadership, and then actually ship the plan. From day one, I revived stale DR tooling and designed a DR strategy for our deployment platform. That work wasn't just architecture diagrams. It required aligning stakeholders who each had a different definition of "acceptable risk."

I learned fast that disaster recovery succeeds or fails long before the incident, based on whether you can get a room to agree on invariants: what must survive, what can degrade, and how we prove it.

Around the same time, I saw a strategic gap that would keep biting us: Infrastructure as Code was not being treated like a first-class product. I initiated the **IaC working group** to push modernization forward, not as a "nice-to-have," but as the only scalable way to keep reliability and security from becoming heroic labor.

### 2022: Resilience Under Constraint

The theme this year was doing more with less. Incidents don't care about headcount. When IP address exhaustion threatened network growth, I built visibility tooling that gave us real insight into network configuration and consumption. That visibility mattered because IP exhaustion is one of those failures that looks "sudden" only to people who weren't watching the counters.

I partnered with a professional services team to build high-performance DR tooling at **a fraction of vendor cost**. The outcome wasn't theoretical: during a production incident, we recovered data in hours rather than the weeks it would have taken before. That's the kind of delta that changes how a company thinks about risk, because it turns DR from "pray and escalate" into an engineered capability.

This year also saw team building. I was a key partner in hiring, pushing for a diverse, high-skill bench and helping keep execution steady while the organization was in motion. The engineers I mentored would go on to lead their own initiatives.

### 2023: Reshaping How We Build

My scope widened from "keep the system upright" to "reshape how we build the system." A major data engineering initiative required the first multi-account production workload. I designed the AWS organizational structure that became the foundation for multi-account, created the cross-account connectivity and VPC architecture, and then trained engineers so the knowledge didn't stay trapped in my head.

I drove a major networking architecture change that delivered multi-million dollar annual cost savings by converting the primary data VPC from Transit Gateway to peering, **without downtime or business interruption**. That wasn't a tweak. Peering can be cleaner and cheaper in the right topology, but it requires discipline in network design and operational habits. We made it work, and the savings proved that architectural rigor can be a profit center, not just a reliability story.

I wrote and owned the **Cloud Architecture Strategy**, establishing the long-term vision for infrastructure investments. And I kept the IaC working group moving, because organizational entropy always tries to turn standards into optional suggestions.

### 2024: Executive Influence and Decision Quality

My impact shifted toward executive influence and decision quality. I led a comprehensive cost analysis for a proposed multi-region architecture. Multi-region is the sort of initiative that can become a prestige project: expensive, complex, and politically hard to stop once momentum builds. Rather than advocating for a specific outcome, I designed alternatives and presented decision-ready options **directly to senior leadership**, with costs, risks, and operational implications attached. I didn't just bring problems; I brought paths forward.

A major deliverable was the **foundational technical design document** for the entire AWS organization. I treated it as a blueprint, not a memo: a durable specification for how to scale a multi-account environment over years, with standards teams can actually follow. Documentation like that is infrastructure too, just made of text instead of routers, because it determines whether the next ten teams repeat the last ten teams' mistakes.

### 2025: Execution at Scale

I spearheaded the **Next-Gen Network Architecture** initiative and delivered foundational components **one month ahead of schedule**. The goal was designing the network to scale toward the company's growth ambitions without letting operational cost and fragility scale at the same rate.

I implemented AWS IPAM and new VPC visibility tooling to reduce the risk of IP exhaustion while supporting substantial network growth. This is the kind of work that prevents the 2 a.m. "why is everything timing out" page months later. The **centralized egress redesign** unlocked significant annual savings, another example of treating cost optimization as an architectural property, not a quarterly scramble.

I kept mentoring as a core output, not a side quest. I guided engineers across teams, led technical workshops, and supported a junior engineer in designing a concurrency feature for our DR tooling that dramatically reduced recovery times for large-scale jobs. That's the multiplier effect I care about: I deliver, and I leave behind stronger engineers and systems that don't require my constant presence to remain correct.

### 2026: Global Connectivity in Production

I led the deployment of **AWS Cloud WAN** into production, delivering cross-region connectivity that became the backbone for regional expansion. A revenue-critical product team was blocked on multi-region availability. I delivered the routing infrastructure they depended on, and it's active in production today.

I built a comprehensive **Terraform module library** for Cloud WAN, with submodules for VPC attachment, Transit Gateway peering, and Direct Connect gateway integration, so future multi-region deployments are standardized rather than one-off. The modules support configurable egress routing with **zero-downtime migration** between NAT gateways, centralized egress via Transit Gateway, and Cloud WAN.

I stood up a dedicated test environment to validate Cloud WAN-based centralized egress end-to-end before touching production. I also automated the deployment pipeline so production network changes use plan/apply workflows instead of manual runs. I coordinated changes across four teams to land Cloud WAN, VPC, and DNS updates in a controlled, auditable, and repeatable way, reducing risk for every future regional expansion.

Looking ahead, the infrastructure patterns I've built for multi-region cloud connectivity, from automated provisioning to zero-downtime migrations, are directly applicable to the emerging AI infrastructure challenge. GPU orchestration, LLM serving, and training pipeline infrastructure require the same disciplined approach to platform engineering that I've practiced for twenty years.

### Provisioning at Hyperscale

Throughout my tenure, I led EC2 provisioning at hyperscale, managing large-scale compute infrastructure and significant storage footprint. I oversaw multi-hierarchy image builder infrastructure and led the immutable infrastructure transition, ensuring the compute foundation could scale with the business.

When network capacity constrained growth, I **advocated directly to cloud provider leadership**, working collaboratively across company boundaries to secure the capacity increase needed.

### The Pattern

What I'm proudest of is not any single deliverable, even the big ones. It's the pattern: I proactively identify architectural risk, champion cost optimization without sacrificing resilience, and build alignment across engineering, security, and leadership so hard changes actually land. The only sustainable scaling strategy is multiplying competent decision-makers, and that's what I optimize for.
